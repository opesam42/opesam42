Potential Energy

Mechanical energy is sub-divided into potential energy and kinetic energy.
Potential Energy is defined as the energy possessed by a body due to its position or its configuration. Potential energy is also known as stored energy.
Gravitational potential energy is related with an object's position, while elastic potential energy has to do with an object's configuration or arrangement.

An object with mass possess potential energy when lifted through a height. The energy possess by a body is proportional to its mass, the height through which it is lifted and the acceleration due to gravity.
P.E = mass × acceleration due to gravity × height
P.E = mgh
Assume that your feet is on the floor and a stone fell from the table edge and hit your feet, ouch!, it hurt, right?. Now compare it with a stone B with the same mass of stone A falling from the ceiling fan. I think that ouch!!!, really hurt. The stones in each instances has stored energy, and have the potential to do work. The reason why stone B hurt than stone A is because it is lifted through a height greater than stone A, therefore, it possess more energy, and will do more work.

The potential energy in a body is equivalent to the work it does when the energy is utilised.
Let take for instance, a simple pendulum and a small tennis ball is set up. The ball is placed at the path through which the pendulum will pass. Now lift the mass of the pendulum and then release it. The pendulum mass hit the ball and the ball moves, record the distance the ball moves through. Now lift the pendulum mass more higher than before, then release it, you will notice the ball moves through a greater distance than before. If the mass of the pendulum is to be replaced with a bigger one, the ball will move farther away than that of the first two instances.
The increased potential energy brings about an equivalent increase in the workdone on the tennis ball.

The following points are captured from the explanation above:

  • An object at a greater height posses more potential energy than an object placed at a lower height.
  • Potential energy of a body is equal to the work done in lifting to such height (if a book on a shelf has a potential energy of 45J, the work done in placing the book on the shelf is 45J
  • Conversely, potential energy of a body at a certain height is equal to the workdone it does when it falls from such height. The book on the shelf would also do a gravitational work of 45J if it falls from the shelf to the ground.

Elastic Potential Energy

This is the energy possessed or stored by an object due to its configuration. This energy is connected with elastic materials like rubber band, string, archer's bow and catapult. This energy is only possesed by an elastic material which is stretched. A stretched bow stores energy and therefore have the potential to do work on an arrow. The more the force applied on the bow, the more the potential energy it stores and the more energy transfered to the arrow in form of kinetic energy.

The elastic potential energy stored in an elastic material is given by:
PE = ½Fe
since F = ke
PE = ½ke2
F - Force applied to the elastic material (N),
e - extension of elastic material (metres),
k - stiffness of the elastic material (Newton per metre N/m).
See Hooke's law

Kinetic Energy

Kinetic energy is the energy an object possessed due to its motion.
In respect to one of the previous example where a stone hit your foot ouch!. Let assume it hit a table. As the stone is released from the height, the potential enregy is gradually converted to kinetic energy, (the value for potential energy decreases while the value for kinetic energy increases until the potential energy reaches zero and the kinetic energy attains its maximum value). At that point, the stone hit the table and a bang! is heard.
In the absence of friction
Potential energy = Kinetic energy = Sound energy

This follows the law of conservation of energy which states that energy can neither be created nor destroyed, but can be converted from one form to another.
Therefore, Potential Energy lost = Kinetic Energy gained

The formulae used to calculate the kinetic energy of an object is:
KE = ½mv2
m = mass of object (kg);
v = velocity or speed of the object (m/s)

Power

Power is defined as the (time) rate of doing work. When one say something is powerful, what jumps to you mind? Maybe it is stronger or huge or can do more work. This does not define power at all as far as time is not included.

Power = force × velocity

How is force × velocity becomes a formulae of power

The expression of power is workdone/time. Since the expression for workdone is force × displacement. The expression for power can be rewritten as (force × displacement)/time.
Since velocity = displacement/time; Therefore, power = force × velocity.

Let take a test.

1. Two students Bimpe and Ola were told to lift ten buckets full of water each, one at a time through a distance of 100 metres. Bimpe was able to do this in just 10 minutes, while Ola finished hers 2 minutes earlier.
Who did the most work? Who was more powerful?

Answer
Who did the most work? Both of them did the same work because they both carried ten buckets each covering the same distance.
Who was more powerful? Ola of course, she was able to do her work in just 8 minutes (2 minutes earlier than the time which Bimpe did her own work finish).

2. Two friends Samuel and John with negligible mass difference ran up a hill at the same time and reached the top at the same time. But Samuel has a bag filled with books.strapped to his back. Who did the most work. Who was more powerful?

Answer
Who did the most work? They both did the same amount of work since they reached the top of the hill at the same time and the difference between their masses is negligible.
Who was more powerful? Samuel is the most powerful of the two, because he had to utilize more force to move the bag along to him, therefore he is stronger than John and hence more powerful.

Example 8

A pump is capable of lifting 5000kg of water through a vertical height of 60 metres in 15 minutes. Calculate the power of the pump. (g = 10m/s2)

Solution

Write out the parameters
mass of water (m) = 5000kg;
height (h) = 60m;
time (t) = 15 minutes
= (15 × 60) seconds
Write out the formulae
Power = Workdone/time
But Workdone = mgh
Power = mgh/t
Substitute then solve
P = (5000 × 10 × 60)/(15 × 60)
= 3333.33 watts
= 3.3 × 103w

Example 9

(g = 10m/s2, 1 horsepower = 746W) NECO 2021
